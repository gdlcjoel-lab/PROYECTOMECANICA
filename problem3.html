Base Case Simulation

Exercise Statement — Section 3.1
Calculations

Consider a rocket characterised by the following parameters: m₀ = 100 kg (initial mass), mₚ = 10 kg (payload mass), T = 1500 N (thrust), Isp = 100 s (specific impulse), Cd = 0.5 (drag coefficient), A_ref = 0.1 m² (cross-sectional area). The rocket is launched vertically from the Earth's surface. Use Euler's method to determine:

aThe velocity v and position r of the rocket as a function of time. Derive the analytical expression for m(t) and v_z(t) during the burn phase (Tsiolkovsky equation with gravitational loss).
bThe maximum height reached by the rocket — both analytically (no drag) and numerically (Euler + ISA).
cThe time of flight t_F and the location of the landing point — compare analytical (no drag) vs numerical results.

Analysis — Questions d to h
Section 3.2 answers
d — Why does drag decrease rapidly with altitude?
Aerodynamic drag F_D = ½·Cd·A·ρ(z)·|v|² is directly proportional to air density ρ(z). As the rocket ascends, the ISA density drops dramatically — at 11 km it is only ~30% of sea-level value (0.364 vs 1.225 kg/m³). This rapid density decrease causes drag to plummet with altitude even though the rocket is still moving fast.
e — What happens to drag at very high altitude?
Beyond ~85 km (exosphere) air density drops to near-zero (<10⁻⁵ kg/m³), so drag effectively vanishes. The rocket enters free-flight: only gravity acts on it. In this model, since our rocket stays below 12 km, drag still approaches zero at apogee because both ρ and |v| are simultaneously small there.
f — What is the range and deviation of the rocket?
Numerical (Euler + ISA): landing at x = −3.044 m (westward), y = 0.000 m → range = 3.044 m. Analytical (no drag): x = −6138.9 m, y = 0 m → range = 6138.9 m. The deviation is purely East-West (Coriolis); North-South displacement is exactly zero at the equator.
g — Coriolis effect vs aerodynamic friction: which matters more?
Drag is far more important. It reduces z_max from ~170 km (analytical) to just 11.3 km — a factor of 15. The Coriolis effect only produces a 3 m lateral drift. Drag impulse ≈ 54,696 N·s vs Coriolis impulse ≈ 77 N·s — a ratio of ~711×.
h — Comparison with u = 1000 m/s, N = 10
Our rocket already has N = m₀/mₚ = 10 and u = g₀·Isp = 980.7 m/s. The hypothetical rocket with u = 1000 m/s and N = 10 is nearly identical — Δv difference = +44.5 m/s (+1.97%). Both reach practically the same altitude; the marginal gain is a fraction of a kilometre. The westward range would also be slightly larger.

"""
Section 3.2 — Base Case Analysis
Plots: altitude | horizontal projection | speed | drag vs time
Questions d–h answered in console output
"""
import numpy as np
from rocket_simulation import (
    simulate_rocket, compute_metrics, print_metrics, plot_base_results,
    T0, ISP, M0, MP, G0, OMEGA_EARTH, density_isa,
)

# Run base case with default parameters
t, r, v, drag, mass, accel = simulate_rocket()
metrics = compute_metrics(t, r, v)
print_metrics(metrics, "BASE CASE  (T=1500 N, ISA, Coriolis ON)")

# Q d — drag decreases because rho(z) drops with altitude
# Q e — at very high altitude rho→0, so drag→0 (free-flight)
# Q f — range and deviation
print(f"Landing x (East) : {metrics['x_land']:+.4f} m")
print(f"Landing y (North): {metrics['y_land']:+.4f} m")
print(f"Horizontal range : {metrics['range']:.4f} m")

# Q g — Coriolis vs drag (impulse comparison)
_trapz = getattr(np, 'trapezoid', np.trapz)
drag_impulse = float(_trapz(drag, t))
coriolis_est = float(2 * OMEGA_EARTH * _trapz(mass * np.abs(v[:, 2]), t))
print(f"Drag impulse     : {drag_impulse:.1f} N·s")
print(f"Coriolis impulse : {coriolis_est:.3f} N·s")
print(f"Ratio drag/Coril : {drag_impulse/coriolis_est:.0f}×")

# Q h — comparison with u=1000 m/s, N=10
u_current = ISP * G0          # 980.7 m/s
dv_current = u_current * np.log(M0 / MP)
dv_comp    = 1000.0 * np.log(10.0)
print(f"Our rocket  Δv = {dv_current:.2f} m/s  (u={u_current:.1f} m/s)")
print(f"Comparison  Δv = {dv_comp:.2f} m/s  (u=1000 m/s)")
print(f"Difference       {dv_comp - dv_current:+.2f} m/s ({100*(dv_comp-dv_current)/dv_current:.2f}%)")

# Interactive 4-panel figure
plot_base_results(t, r, v, drag,
                  title="Section 3.2 — Base Case (T=1500N, ISA, Coriolis ON)")
