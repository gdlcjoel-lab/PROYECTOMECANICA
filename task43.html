Exercise Statement
Section 4.3 of the project brief

Replace the ISA atmospheric density model with a simplified single-exponential model: ρ(z) = ρ₀ · exp(−z / H), where ρ₀ = 1.225 kg/m³ and H = 8500 m. Keep all other parameters identical to the base case.

Q1Compare the maximum altitudes reached under the ISA model and the exponential model. Explain the difference in terms of density profiles.
Q2How does the aerodynamic drag evolve differently over time for each model? Identify the altitude range where the models diverge most.
Q3Which model is more physically realistic? Justify with reference to the atmospheric layer structure.

Analysis
Answers derived from simulation
Q1 — Maximum altitude comparison
ISA: 11.278 km · Exponential: 11.765 km (Δ = +0.487 km). The exponential model predicts ~8% lower density in the 5–15 km range, reducing drag during the powered phase and early coast, allowing the rocket to reach a higher apogee.
Q2 — Drag evolution
ISA shows a smoother drag decrease with a subtle slope change at the tropopause (11 km). The exponential model produces consistently lower drag in the 7–20 km range — visible as a gap in the drag vs time plot from roughly t = 30–70 s. Both converge at sea level (ρ₀ identical) and at very high altitude (both → 0).
Q3 — Physical realism
ISA is far more realistic. It captures the temperature inversion at the tropopause, the isothermal lower stratosphere, and the warming in the mesosphere — all validated against balloon and satellite measurements to <1% up to 85 km. The exponential model is a convenient analytical approximation valid only in the lower troposphere.

"""
Task 4.3 — Atmosphere Model Comparison
ISA multi-layer vs single-exponential rho(z) = rho0 * exp(-z/H)
"""
import numpy as np
from rocket_simulation import (simulate_rocket, compute_metrics, print_metrics,
    density_isa, density_exponential, RHO0, H_SCALE)
from plot_utils import plot_comparison

DENSITY_CASES = [density_isa, density_exponential]
LABELS        = ['ISA (multi-layer)', 'Exponential ρ₀·exp(−z/H)']
COLORS        = ['#0D2B55', '#B07D2A']
LINESTYLES    = ['--', '-']

results, metrics_list = [], []
for density_func, label in zip(DENSITY_CASES, LABELS):
    t, r, v, drag, mass, accel = simulate_rocket(density_func=density_func)
    results.append((t, r, v, drag, mass, accel))
    metrics_list.append(compute_metrics(t, r, v))

m_isa, m_exp = metrics_list
print(f"ISA   z_max = {m_isa['z_max']/1000:.3f} km")
print(f"Exp   z_max = {m_exp['z_max']/1000:.3f} km")
print(f"Delta      = {(m_exp['z_max']-m_isa['z_max'])/1000:+.3f} km")

# Density comparison table
for alt_km in [0, 2, 5, 8, 11, 15, 20]:
    ri = density_isa(alt_km*1000); re = density_exponential(alt_km*1000)
    print(f"{alt_km:2d}km  ISA={ri:.3e}  Exp={re:.3e}  ratio={re/ri:.3f}")

# Interactive plot: altitude, drag, speed, air density along trajectory
plot_comparison(panels=[...], labels=LABELS, colors=COLORS,
                suptitle='Task 4.3 — ISA vs Exponential Atmosphere')
