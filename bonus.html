Bonus — ECI 3-Stage Simulation

3-Stage Rocket to the ISS

Design and simulate a Soyuz-type 3-stage rocket that reaches the International Space Station at 408 km orbit. Full ECI numerical simulation including gravity turn, staging, Hohmann transfer, circularisation and orbital rendezvous.

Target: ISS · 408 km LEO ECI · Leapfrog + Euler hybrid 54 200 kg · 500 kg payload Hohmann transfer · Δv budget
📋
Exercise Statement
Bonus section of the project brief

Design a 3-stage rocket (inspired by the Soyuz-FG architecture) capable of reaching the International Space Station (ISS) at approximately 408 km altitude. Simulate the full mission numerically using the Earth-Centred Inertial (ECI) frame with the Euler integration scheme, extended with the leapfrog (Störmer–Verlet) integrator for orbital coast phases.

1Define the rocket parameters (thrust, Isp, propellant and structural mass per stage) and compute the ideal Δv budget using the Tsiolkovsky equation for each stage.
2Simulate the complete mission: atmospheric ascent with gravity turn, staging, suborbital coast, circularisation burn, Hohmann transfer to ISS altitude, and final circularisation.
3Report key mission milestones (stage separation times and altitudes, parking orbit elements, final orbital speed and period). Document all physical simplifications adopted.
4Justify the use of the leapfrog integrator for coast and orbit phases versus the Euler integrator for powered phases.
⚙️
Rocket Architecture
Soyuz-type · scaled for a 500 kg payload
Stage 1 — booster (LOX/Kero)
T = 800 kN · Isp = 290 s · 40 t prop
Stage 2 — upper stage (LH₂/LOX)
T = 200 kN · Isp = 348 s · 8 t prop
Stage 3 — manoeuvre / RCS
T = 2 kN · Isp = 300 s · 500 kg prop
Total initial mass
54 200 kg · Payload 500 kg
Drag cutoff (Kármán line)
100 km — no atmospheric drag above
Gravity turn profile
3 km → 80 km (0° → 90° pitch)
Δv budget (Tsiolkovsky, ideal): Stage 1: 3 809 m/s · Stage 2: 5 235 m/s · Stage 3: 1 586 m/s · Total: 10 630 m/s. Required for LEO with losses: ~9 400 m/s — the budget provides sufficient margin.
🛸
Mission Phases
6 phases from launch to ISS docking
① Stage 1 burn — atmospheric ascent · t ≈ 0–142 s
800 kN with progressive gravity turn from 3 km to 80 km. Stage 1 jettisoned at t ≈ 142 s, h ≈ 86 km, saving 4 t of structural mass.
② Stage 2 burn — orbit insertion · t ≈ 142–281 s
200 kN continues the gravity turn in vacuum. Burnout at t ≈ 281 s, h ≈ 171 km. Stage 2 jettisoned.
③ Coast — suborbital arc to apogee · t ≈ 281–981 s
Free ballistic coast with leapfrog integrator (symplectic, conserves orbital energy). Apogee reached at h ≈ 265 km.
④ Circularisation + Hohmann burn 1 · t ≈ 981–1081 s
Stage 3 burns prograde at apogee to circularise at h ≈ 266 km. Immediately after: Hohmann burn 1 (+41 m/s prograde) raises apogee to 408 km.
⑤ Hohmann transfer coast · t ≈ 1081–3937 s
Coast on transfer ellipse (leapfrog) from 266 km perigee to 408 km apogee.
⑥ ISS orbit + rendezvous · t ≈ 3937–9504 s
Hohmann burn 2 (+43 m/s) circularises at ISS altitude. One full orbital period (92.8 min) completed → docking confirmed.

"""
bonus_analysis.py — Entry point for the 3-stage ISS mission.
Run:  python bonus_analysis.py
Requires: bonus_multistage.py, bonus_plot.py (same directory)
"""
from bonus_multistage import simulate_multistage, print_mission_summary
from bonus_plot import plot_mission, plot_orbit
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Full ECI simulation: Euler (atmosphere) + Leapfrog (vacuum/orbit)
# dt_atm = 0.1 s (h < 100 km)  |  dt_space = 5 s (burns in vacuum)
# dt_orbit = 1 s (coast + ISS orbit, leapfrog)
data = simulate_multistage(dt_atm=0.1, dt_space=5.0)

# Console: stage separations, orbital elements, Tsiolkovsky Dv budget
print_mission_summary(data)

# Figure 1: 4-panel interactive timeline with time slider
# Panels: altitude [km] | speed [m/s] | mass [kg] | altitude vs speed
# Phases colour-coded: Stage 1 (blue) → Stage 2 → Coast → Circ → Hohmann → ISS
fig1 = plot_mission(data)

# Figure 2: 2-D ECI x-y orbital view
# Shows Earth disc, ISS target orbit (dashed) and full trajectory by phase
fig2 = plot_orbit(data)

plt.show()
