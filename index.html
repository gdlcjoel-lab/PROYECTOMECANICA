Sensitivity Tasks

Thrust Variation

Same propellant (90 kg) and same total impulse. Thrust is varied to analyse its influence on the maximum altitude reached.

KEY INSIGHT

T=3000 N reaches less altitude than T=1500 N despite the higher thrust. Faster ascent through dense air → greater drag losses. The thrust-altitude relationship is non-monotonic.

Coriolis Effect

Comparison between the real trajectory (with Earth's rotation) and the trajectory on a static planet. Launch from the equator.

KEY INSIGHT

The Coriolis effect deflects the rocket 3.04 m westward at landing. North deviation is exactly 0. Drag is 711× more significant than Coriolis (impulses: 54,696 N·s vs 77 N·s).

Atmosphere Models

Comparison between the multi-layer ISA model (standard reference) and the simplified exponential model used in rapid analyses.

KEY INSIGHT

Exponential model is 7.6% less dense at 0–11 km → less drag → +487 m apogee. Above 20 km it overestimates density by 32%. ISA is more realistic (error <1% up to 85 km).

Propellant Mass

Fixed thrust T=1500 N. Propellant mass is varied to analyse its effect on final altitude and ascent dynamics.

KEY INSIGHT

More propellant → lower initial acceleration but much longer burn. Apogee altitude grows superlinearly with propellant mass.
